The Story of Jonny Clayton

If you ever needed proof that it's never too late, look no further than Jonny Clayton. The Welshman spent years working as a plasterer, juggling the day job with the darts, before catching fire in his mid-40s and becoming one of the most beloved players on the circuit.

Born on 4 October 1974 in Llanelli and based in the village of Pontyberem, Clayton has been throwing since 1995, coming up through the BDO before earning his PDC Tour Card in 2015. For a good while he was a solid, respected pro without a major to his name — the sort of grafter every fan has a soft spot for. The nickname "The Ferret" was handed to him by his great mate and Welsh World Cup partner Gerwyn Price, and it stuck.

Jonny Clayton in action — the Welsh grafter who lit up the oche in his magical 2021 season.

Then came 2021, and what a year it was. Clayton won four PDC major titles in a single season — the Masters, the Premier League, the World Grand Prix and the World Series Finals. For a player who'd waited so long for his first big trophy, suddenly winning everything in sight was the stuff of fairy tales. He'd already tasted glory with Wales, winning the World Cup of Darts in 2020 alongside Price, and the pair would do it again in 2023.

That golden run lifted him into the world's top five, a position he's held with real consistency since. He's reached the World Championship quarter-finals in both 2023 and 2026, proving he's no flash in the pan. The Jonny Clayton darts story is a brilliant reminder that hard graft and self-belief pay off.

Playing Style & Technique

Jonny Clayton is the model of a steady, big-match competitor. He's not the fastest thrower on tour, nor does he go searching for flashy averages — instead he plays a controlled, consistent game built on relentless scoring and ice-cold finishing when it matters.

The Ferret in full cry — Clayton's big-stage temperament makes him a nightmare to beat in the latter stages of majors.

His real strength is temperament. Clayton thrives in the heat of a major, and his 2021 haul showed a man who simply doesn't wilt under pressure — that World Grand Prix run, with finish after finish under the lights, was the perfect example. When he gets his nose in front, he's a tough man to peg back.

There's also the doubles. Clayton's a clinical finisher who makes the most of his chances, and it's that efficiency on the outer ring that's turned tight matches into trophies. Add in the experience of a player who's seen it all, and you've got one of the most dependable big-game operators in the sport.
