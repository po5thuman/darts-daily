The Story of Gian van Veen

Every era throws up a next-big-thing, and in the new wave of darting talent, Gian van Veen is right at the front of the queue. The Dutchman — known to everyone as GVV — has climbed the ranks at a rate of knots, and he's done it on raw quality.

Born Pieter Gerard van Veen on 23 April 2002 in the small Dutch village of Poederoijen, he picked up the darts in 2015 and never looked back. The youth circuit didn't stand a chance: he topped the 2023 Development Tour rankings with six titles in a single year, then went and won the PDC World Youth Championship in both 2024 and 2025 — back-to-back, a marker of just how dominant he was at that level.

Gian van Veen in action — the Dutch youngster tipped as a future World Champion.

His first deep run on the big stage came at the 2023 European Championship, where he reached the semi-finals as a relative unknown. By 2025 he'd kicked on properly, winning his first PDC ranking title at Players Championship 6 — and then the big one. At the 2025 European Championship he claimed his maiden PDC major, defeating none other than Luke Humphries in the final.

The momentum carried into the biggest event of all. At the 2026 PDC World Championship, van Veen battled all the way to the final, only to come up against the relentless Luke Littler. Runner-up at the Worlds at 23, and a major champion already — that's a frightening trajectory.

Now sitting at world number three, GVV is no longer a prospect. He's the real deal, and the Gian van Veen darts story looks set to run and run.

Playing Style & Technique

Gian van Veen plays the kind of darts that gets crowds on their feet. He's a quick, fluid thrower with a lovely natural rhythm, and his lighter 21g darts let him keep that snappy tempo going without forcing it. When he's on song, he scores in big, relentless bursts.

GVV celebrating on the big stage — composure and quality well beyond his years.

What stands out for a player so young is his temperament. Beating Luke Humphries in a major final, then reaching a World Championship final, takes serious bottle — and van Veen handles those moments like a seasoned pro. He doesn't get flustered, he trusts his scoring, and he's developing a clinical edge on the doubles to match.

With his blend of speed, power and a cool head, GVV has all the tools to be a fixture at the business end of majors for the next decade. The ceiling here is sky-high.
