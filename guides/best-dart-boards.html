Dart Board Buying Guide — What to Look For

Not sure which board is right for you? Here's what to consider before buying:

1

Material — Bristle vs Electronic vs Cork

Bristle (sisal) is the only choice for serious players. The compressed sisal fibres are self-healing — when you pull a dart out, the fibres close back up. Electronic boards are fine for casual fun but aren't used professionally. Cork and paper boards should be avoided entirely — they wear out extremely fast.

2

Wiring — Thinner Is Better

The metal wires that divide segments are the #1 cause of bounce-outs. Thinner wiring means more scoring surface and fewer frustrating bounces. Budget boards have thicker, rounder wires. Premium boards like the Blade 6 have ultra-thin, blade-shaped wires angled to guide darts into the board.

3

Bullseye — Staple-Free Is Essential

Look for a staple-free bullseye. Older and cheaper boards use metal staples to hold the bull in place, which cause bounce-outs. All quality modern boards use a staple-free design.

4

Number Ring — Removable Is Important

A removable number ring lets you rotate the board to spread wear evenly across all segments. Without this, the triple 20 area will wear out much faster than the rest of the board. All boards on our list have removable number rings.

5

Your Skill Level & Budget

Be honest about how often you'll play:

  • Casual/beginner (play once a week) — Viper Shot King
  • Regular player (play 2-3 times a week) — Winmau Blade 6
  • Serious/competitive (play daily) — Blade 6 Dual Core or Triple Core

How to Make Your Dart Board Last Longer

A quality dartboard is an investment. Here's how to get the most life out of it:

🔄

Rotate the board regularly

Remove the number ring and rotate the board by a few segments every 1-2 weeks. This spreads wear evenly across the entire surface instead of destroying the triple 20 area.

☀️

Keep it out of direct sunlight

UV light fades the colours and dries out the sisal fibres, making them less self-healing. Mount your board away from windows or areas with strong direct sunlight.

💧

Avoid moisture and humidity

Don't mount your board in a garage, shed or bathroom. Moisture causes the sisal to swell and warp, ruining the playing surface. A dry, indoor room is ideal.

🎯

Use sharp darts

Blunt dart points tear the sisal fibres instead of parting them cleanly. Keep your dart points smooth and sharp (not needle-sharp — slightly rounded is best) using a dart sharpener.

🚫

Never use water or cleaning products

Don't spray anything on your dartboard. The sisal fibres will absorb the liquid and swell. If your board is dusty, use a dry cloth or soft brush to gently wipe it down.

💡 How Long Should a Dart Board Last?

With proper care and regular rotation, a quality bristle board should last:
Budget board: 6-12 months of regular use
Winmau Blade 6: 1-2 years of regular use
Blade 6 Triple Core: 2-3 years of regular use

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best dart board to buy?

The Winmau Blade 6 is our top pick and widely considered the best dartboard you can buy. It's used in all PDC professional events, features ultra-thin wiring for minimal bounce-outs and offers excellent durability at a reasonable price.

What dart board do professionals use?

Professional PDC tournaments use the Winmau Blade 6. The World Championship at Alexandra Palace, the Premier League and all major PDC events feature the Blade 6 as the official board.

What is the difference between a bristle and electronic dart board?

Bristle boards are made from compressed sisal fibres and use steel tip darts. They're self-healing and used in all professional play. Electronic boards are plastic with tiny holes for soft tip darts. They auto-calculate scores but are less durable and not used professionally.

How long does a dart board last?

A quality bristle board lasts 1-3 years with regular use. Extend its life by rotating regularly, keeping it away from sunlight and moisture, and using sharp darts.

Do I need a dartboard surround?

A surround isn't essential but is highly recommended for home setups. It protects your wall from stray darts, especially when you're starting out. Most surrounds cost between €40 - €600.

What size is a standard dart board?

A standard regulation dartboard has a diameter of 45.1cm (17.75 inches). This is the size used in all professional tournaments and is the standard across all major manufacturers.

Is the Blade 6 Dual Core worth the extra money?

If you play 2-3+ times per week, yes. The dual-core technology provides noticeably better dart retention and lasts longer. For casual players who play once a week or less, the standard Blade 6 is more than enough.
