Why Choose an Electronic Dart Board?

Electronic dart boards have come a long way from the cheap, noisy boards of the past. Modern electronic and smart boards offer features that traditional bristle boards simply can't match:

  • Automatic scoring — no mental maths, no arguments. The board tracks every throw and calculates scores instantly
  • Built-in games — most boards come with 20-40+ games including 501, Cricket, Around the Clock and party games
  • Safe for homes — soft tip darts won't damage walls, floors or furniture if you miss (and you will miss)
  • Kid-friendly — soft tips are much safer than steel tip darts for families with young children
  • Online play — smart boards let you play against opponents worldwide from your living room
  • Stats tracking — smart boards track your averages, checkout percentages and improvement over time
  • LED displays — bright, colourful scoring displays that look great in game rooms and man caves
💡 Who Should Buy an Electronic Board?

Electronic dart boards are perfect for casual players, families, beginners and anyone who wants hassle-free scoring. If you're training seriously for league or competition darts, a traditional bristle board is the better choice since all professional events use bristle boards with steel tip darts.

Buying Guide — What to Look For

Not sure which type of electronic board is right for you? Here's what to consider before buying:

1

Smart Board or Standard Electronic?

This is the biggest decision. Smart boards (Gran Board 3, Dash) connect to your phone via Bluetooth and offer app scoring, online play and detailed stats. Standard electronic boards (Arachnid, Viper, Fat Cat, Winmau, WIN.MAX) have built-in scoring displays and work independently. Smart boards cost more but offer a significantly richer experience.

2

Number of Games

All electronic boards come with built-in games. More isn't always better — you'll mostly play 501, 301 and Cricket. But having variety is nice for parties and trying new formats. The Viper Ion leads with 48 games while the Winmau Ton Machine offers 21 focused essentials.

3

Board Size — Regulation Matters

Look for boards with a 15.5 inch target face — this is the regulation size used in all competitive darts. Smaller boards might be cheaper but they won't help you develop accuracy that transfers to a real board. All 7 boards on our list are regulation size or close to it.

4

Spider Wire Thickness

The spider wires are the dividers between segments. Thinner wires = fewer bounce-outs. Premium boards like the Gran Board 3 and Arachnid Cricket Pro have the thinnest wires. Budget boards tend to have thicker wires which cause more bounce-outs.

5

Power Source

Some boards run on batteries, some need mains power and some offer both. Battery-powered boards are more flexible for placement but need regular battery changes. Mains-powered boards are more reliable but need to be near a power outlet. Boards with both options give you the most flexibility.

6

Noise Level

Electronic boards are louder than bristle boards because soft tip darts hitting plastic segments creates more noise. If you live in a flat or play late at night, noise matters. Smart boards tend to be quieter than standard electronic boards. The Gran Board 3 is among the quietest options.

7

Included Accessories

Check what comes in the box. Most boards include a basic set of darts but quality varies. The WIN.MAX stands out with 12 darts and 100 extra tips included. The Fat Cat Mercury includes a full cabinet. Some boards include nothing extra — you'll need to buy darts separately.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best electronic dart board?

The Gran Board 3 is the best electronic dart board in 2026. It features Bluetooth connectivity, a companion app with online play, automatic scoring and a large global community. For a budget option, the GRANBOARD Dash offers the same app experience at a lower price.

What is the difference between a smart board and an electronic board?

A standard electronic board has built-in games and displays scores on an LCD/LED screen. A smart board connects to your phone or tablet via Bluetooth, uses a companion app for scoring and statistics, and supports online multiplayer. Smart boards offer a richer experience but cost more.

Are electronic dart boards good for beginners?

Yes, electronic dart boards are excellent for beginners. They handle all scoring automatically, use soft tip darts which are safer, and include multiple built-in games that help new players learn different formats. They remove the intimidation of manual scoring so beginners can focus on throwing. Check out our How to Score Darts guide to learn the scoring basics.

Do electronic dart boards use soft tip or steel tip darts?

Electronic dart boards use soft tip (plastic tip) darts. The board surface has small holes that soft tips press into, triggering sensors to register your score. Never use steel tip darts on an electronic board — they will damage the surface and sensors. For steel tip darts, use a bristle dartboard instead.

Can you play online with an electronic dart board?

Only smart boards with Bluetooth or WiFi support online play. The Gran Board 3 and GRANBOARD Dash both connect to the GRAN BOARD app for worldwide online play. Standard electronic boards without connectivity only support local play.

How long do electronic dart boards last?

A quality electronic dart board typically lasts 2-5 years with regular use. The main wear point is the board surface where dart tips press into the holes. Higher-end boards use more durable materials and last longer. For comparison, bristle boards typically last 3-8 years.

Are electronic dart boards as good as bristle boards?

They serve different purposes. Bristle boards are used in professional darts and offer the most authentic experience with steel tip darts. Electronic boards are better for casual players, families and beginners because they score automatically and use safer soft tip darts. Many players own both — a bristle board for practice and an electronic board for fun.

What should I look for when buying an electronic dart board?

Key features to consider: Bluetooth connectivity (for app scoring and online play), number of games, board size (15.5" regulation), spider wire thickness (thinner = fewer bounce-outs), power source (battery vs mains), noise level and included accessories (darts, tips, cabinet).

How far should you stand from an electronic dart board?

The official throwing distance for soft tip darts is 8 feet (2.44m) — slightly further than the steel tip distance of 7ft 9¼in. The board height remains the same at 5ft 8in (1.73m) to the bullseye. See our full Dart Board Distance & Height guide for detailed setup instructions.

Are electronic dart boards noisy?

Electronic boards are louder than bristle boards because soft tip darts hitting plastic segments creates more impact noise. Smart boards tend to be quieter than standard electronic boards. If noise is a concern, the Gran Board 3 is among the quietest options. Adding a dartboard surround can also help reduce noise from missed darts hitting the wall.
