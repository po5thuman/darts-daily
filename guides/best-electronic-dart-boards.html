Why Choose an Electronic Dart Board?

If you'd asked me about electronic dart boards five years ago, I'd have said don't bother. They were clunky, inaccurate and felt like toys. But the newer ones? Genuinely impressive. Here's what makes them worth considering now:

  • Automatic scoring — no mental maths, no arguments about whether that was a treble or a single. The board handles everything
  • Built-in games — most come loaded with 20-40+ games. 501, Cricket, Around the Clock and a bunch of party games you've never heard of
  • Safe for homes — soft tip darts won't destroy your walls, floors or furniture when you inevitably miss. And you will miss
  • Kid-friendly — plastic tips are much safer than steel points if you've got little ones running around
  • Online play — the smart boards let you play against people worldwide from your living room. It's properly addictive
  • Stats tracking — smart boards track your averages, checkout percentages and show you exactly how you're improving (or not)
  • LED displays — bright, colourful scoring that looks great in a game room or man cave
💡 Who Should Buy an Electronic Board?

Electronic dart boards are ideal for casual players, families, beginners and anyone who just wants hassle-free fun. If you're training seriously for league or competition darts though, you'll want a traditional bristle board — that's what every professional event uses.

Buying Guide — What Actually Matters

There's a lot of marketing nonsense around electronic dart boards. Here's what you should actually care about when choosing one:

1

Smart Board or Standard Electronic?

This is the big one. Smart boards (Gran Board 3, Dash) connect to your phone and give you app scoring, online play and detailed stats. Standard electronic boards (everything else on this list) work on their own with built-in displays. Smart boards cost more but the experience is genuinely different. If you'll play regularly, the extra money is worth it. If it's mainly for parties and casual fun, save the cash and go standard.

2

Number of Games

Be honest with yourself here. You're going to play 501, Cricket and maybe Around the Clock. That's what everyone plays. Having 48 games sounds impressive on the box but you'll probably try 5 of them and stick with your favourites. Don't let game count be the deciding factor — the Winmau has 21 games and that's plenty.

3

Board Size — Get Regulation

Look for boards with a 15.5 inch target face — that's the regulation size used in competition. Smaller boards might save you a few quid but your accuracy won't transfer if you ever play on a proper board. All 7 boards on this list are regulation size or very close to it.

4

Spider Wire Thickness

These are the dividers between segments. Thinner wires = fewer bounce-outs. It's that simple. The Gran Board 3 and Arachnid Cricket Pro have the thinnest wires. Budget boards tend to have chunkier wires which means more darts bouncing off the dividers. Frustrating, but expected at lower price points.

5

Power Source

Battery boards are great because you can hang them anywhere. No messing around with extension leads. But you'll be changing batteries, which gets annoying. Mains-powered boards are more reliable but you need a socket nearby. Boards that offer both give you the most flexibility — start with batteries, switch to mains when you find the right spot.

6

Noise Level

This one catches people off guard. Electronic boards are louder than bristle boards — soft tip darts hitting plastic makes a proper clack. If you're in a flat with thin walls, or you like playing late at night, this matters. Smart boards tend to be quieter. The Gran Board 3 is one of the quietest. Budget boards are usually the loudest.

7

What's in the Box

Some boards come with everything. The WIN.MAX gives you 12 darts and 100 spare tips. The Fat Cat Mercury includes a full wooden cabinet. Others come with the bare minimum — board and a couple of basic darts. Check what's included so you know if you need to budget for extras.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best electronic dart board?

The Gran Board 3. It's got the best app, the best online community, the best stats tracking and it plays really well. If it's out of your budget, the GRANBOARD Dash uses the same app and costs less — it's the smart choice if you want the smart experience without paying full whack.

What is the difference between a smart board and an electronic board?

A standard electronic board has games built in and shows scores on a display on the board. A smart board connects to your phone via Bluetooth and does everything through an app — scoring, stats, online multiplayer, the lot. Think of it like the difference between a basic phone and a smartphone. Both make calls, but one does a lot more.

Are electronic dart boards good for beginners?

They're brilliant for beginners. The board handles all the scoring so you can just focus on throwing. No mental maths, no arguments about who hit what. The soft tip darts are safer around the house too, especially with kids about. And most boards come loaded with different games so you can try everything while you're learning.

Do electronic dart boards use soft tip or steel tip darts?

Soft tip only. The plastic tips press into tiny holes on the board to register your score. Don't throw steel tip darts at an electronic board — you'll damage the surface and wreck the sensors. If you want steel tips, get a bristle dartboard.

Can you play online with an electronic dart board?

Only if it's a smart board with Bluetooth. The Gran Board 3 and GRANBOARD Dash both connect to the GRAN BOARD app which lets you play against people all over the world. Standard electronic boards without Bluetooth are local play only — you and whoever's in the room.

How long do electronic dart boards last?

Roughly 2-5 years with regular use. The surface gradually wears down as dart tips press into the same holes over and over. Higher-end boards use tougher materials and last longer. Budget boards show wear sooner but they're cheap enough to replace. For comparison, a good bristle board lasts 3-8 years.

Are electronic dart boards as good as bristle boards?

Different tools for different jobs. Bristle boards are what the pros use — they're in every PDC event and every pub league. Electronic boards are better for casual players and families because they handle scoring and use safer darts. A lot of players end up owning both. Bristle for practice, electronic for fun.

What should I look for when buying an electronic dart board?

The things that actually matter: does it have Bluetooth for app scoring and online play? How thin are the spider wires — thinner means fewer bounce-outs? Is it regulation size — 15.5 inch target face? What power does it need — battery or mains? How loud is it? And what comes in the box — some boards include darts and accessories, others give you the bare minimum.

How far should you stand from an electronic dart board?

The official soft tip throwing distance is 8 feet (2.44m) — slightly further than steel tip which is 7ft 9¼in. The board height stays the same at 5ft 8in (1.73m) to the bullseye. Check our full Dart Board Distance & Height guide for step-by-step setup instructions.

Are electronic dart boards noisy?

Louder than bristle boards, yes. Soft tip darts hitting a plastic surface makes a noticeable clack. Smart boards like the Gran Board 3 tend to be quieter than standard electronic boards. If noise is a concern — flatmates, thin walls, late night sessions — go for a smart board or add a dartboard surround to dampen the sound from missed darts.
